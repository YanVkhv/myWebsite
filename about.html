Let me introduce myself...

I'm just a regular 29-year-old, born in Leuven. After graduating from highschool I wanted to study geography at university, mainly because geography was my best subject in school. I've always been interested in computers too, both hardware and software, but I once got terrible advice from a senior, which made me rethink taking ICT courses. After one year of Geography I realized that this wasn't the subject for me, especially the biology, chemistry and physics classes that came with it. I reoriented the year after and chose a bachelor in Sinology which I completed. After graduating we all had the opportunity to apply for a scholarship in either China or Taiwan. I chose Taiwan, studied Chinese for another 6 months and then got stuck there for 3 years doing translating work. Taiwan is also where I met my awesome girlfriend. Living in Taiwan is great and all, but it's not future-proof. Wanting a more stable life and safeguarding our future, I decided to follow my dream of becoming a developer and returning to Belgium.

Here are some fun facts about Taiwan:

  • You can enjoy free Wi-Fi all over the capital city
  • Taipei's public transportation is heaven, the MRT is super clean and punctual
  • There are 24/7 convenience stores at every corner of the street, here you can buy anything you'd need, mail packages, print documents, book cinema tickets, recharge phones etc.
  • When you hear a melody play in the streets, it won't be an icecream truck but a garbage truck
  • Taiwan is slightly bigger than Belgium, but has a population of more than 23 million people
  • Most Taiwanese girls like having a pale skin, so they carry umbrellas to protect their skin against the sun
  • Taiwan gets hit by approximately 3 typhoons each year
  • Most Taiwanese don't like to walk in the rain, they're afraid the acid rain will damage the hair and skin
  • Every receipt you get after a purchase acts as a lottery ticket
